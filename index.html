Tiered Usage Classification

TierCategoryExamplesApproval
Tier 1 Unrestricted Code completion, documentation, test generation, refactoring suggestions, commit messages None required
Tier 2 Supervised Architecture decisions, security-sensitive code, database migrations, API design Peer review + senior sign-off

Key Policy Pillars

🔒 Data ProtectionPII/PHI/PCI-DSS boundary rules. Data residency for GDPR. What goes to external APIs vs. internal models.

[ASSESS]

Developer AI Maturity Model

Every developer is assessed against this model at program start, then reassessed quarterly. Training pathways are personalized based on current level.

0

Unaware

Has not used any AI coding tools. May have heard of ChatGPT but hasn't applied it to engineering work. Needs awareness + hands-on introduction.

1

Explorer

Uses AI for simple completions. Accepts output uncritically. Doesn't iterate on prompts. Treats AI as a search engine replacement. Needs structured prompt training + critical evaluation skills.

2

Practitioner

Crafts effective prompts with context, constraints, and examples. Reviews and edits AI output. Uses AI for tests, docs, refactoring. Understands model limitations. Target by Month 3.

3

Integrator

Builds AI into personal workflows end-to-end. Chains multi-step operations. Has a personal prompt library. Mentors Level 0-1 colleagues. Measures their own productivity gains. Target by Month 6.

4

Architect

Designs agentic workflows and multi-agent systems. Builds custom tools/agents for the team. Evaluates new AI tools. Contributes to AI strategy. Mentors at scale. Target for power users by Month 9.

[RESISTANCE]

Handling Developer Resistance

Many senior developers will be skeptical. This is healthy. Your job is not to suppress skepticism — it's to channel it into rigorous evaluation. Here's how:

Common Objections & Evidence-Based Responses

❌ "AI generates garbage code"
✅ Live demo: bad prompts → bad output, then good prompts → good output. The tool amplifies skill, it doesn't replace it. Show the difference a well-crafted system prompt makes.
❌ "It'll replace us"
✅ Data shows AI + human consistently outperforms AI alone. Developers who use AI are more valuable, not less. Frame it as career insurance, not a threat.
❌ "I'm faster without it"
✅ Challenge accepted: timed head-to-head on real boilerplate tasks. Most skeptics convert after seeing 30%+ time savings. Let the evidence do the talking.
❌ "It's just hype"
✅ Agree that hype exists. Then show concrete enterprise data and your own internal metrics. Separate signal from noise together — that's exactly what smart engineers do.
❌ "Security/IP concerns"
✅ Walk through the corporate policy. Show approved tools, data boundaries, audit trails. Address the real concern with real controls — not dismissal.
❌ "I don't want to learn another tool"
✅ This is a meta-skill, not a tool. The skill is working with AI. Tools change every 6 months; the reasoning skill compounds forever.

Engagement Strategy for Resistant Seniors

The 5 Rules
  1. Respect their expertise — AI is a force multiplier for their skills, not a replacement.
  2. Start with their pain points — Ask what they hate doing. Show AI solving that specific thing.
  3. Give them agency — Let them choose their first AI project. Forced adoption backfires.
  4. Leverage peer proof — One respected senior who's converted beats any training deck.
  5. Make Phase 1 opt-in — Mandates breed resentment. Mandate only after internal proof exists.

Success Metrics & ROI Framework

Leading Indicators (Monthly)

  • % of developers at each maturity level (target: 80% at Level 2+ by Month 6)
  • AI tool daily active users / total developers
  • AI-assisted PRs merged per week
  • Training module completion rates
  • Champion office hour attendance

Lagging Indicators (Quarterly)

  • Developer velocity (story points, cycle time) — before vs. after
  • Code quality (bug density, test coverage) — before vs. after
  • Developer satisfaction survey (AI-specific questions)
  • Time-to-onboard for new hires
  • Internal AI agents built and deployed

ROI Calculation

Annual Program Cost  = Training + Licenses + Headcount + External Services
Productivity Gain    = (Avg hours saved/dev/week) × (Hourly cost) × (# devs) × 50 weeks
Quality Improvement  = (Reduction in prod incidents) × (Avg incident cost)
ROI                  = (Total Gain − Total Cost) / Total Cost × 100%
